Remember that similarity in characteristics resulting from a common ancestor is known as homology. Convergent evolution is the process whereby organisms that are not closely related to each other independently evolve similar traits as a result of having to adapt to similar environments. So, the fact that the two species share many proteins and that these shared proteins are encoded by genes with identical DNA sequences would provide strong evidence that the two species evolved from a recent common ancestor, and that the similar-looking structure is thus due to homology. The fact that the two species live far apart does not provide convincing evidence that the two species are not closely related as its possible for members of some species to travel long distances. Also, the fact that the structures are similar in size and that both species are well adapted to their environment doesnt tell us whether the two species are or are not closely related to each other.
