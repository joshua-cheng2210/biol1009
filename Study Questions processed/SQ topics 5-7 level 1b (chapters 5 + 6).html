MgCl2 and NaCl are both salts that will dissociate into their constituent ions when they come in contact with water.  So, the 1 mole of MgCl2 will dissociate to form 1 mole of Mg++ and 2 moles of Cl- and the 1 mole of NaCl will dissociate to form 1 mole of Na+
and 1 mole of Cl-.  That means that, immediately after the solutions are added to the two sides of the tube, there will be a higher concentration of solute molecules on side A than on side B, so side A will be hypertonic compared with side B.  The fact that there will be more solute molecules on side A also means that more of the water molecules on side A will be interacting with solute molecules and therefore there will be fewer free water molecules on side A.

In this case, the concentration of glucose is the same on both sides of the tube. So, the concentration of free water molecules is the same on both sides of the tube. Free water molecules will still cross the membrane, but the rate at which free water molecules cross from side A to side B will be equal to the rate at which free water molecules cross from side B to side A. So, free water molecules WILL still cross the membrane, but there will be not NET flow of water between the two sides of the tube.

Nice work!

Channel proteins carry out “facilitated diffusion”. In facilitated diffusion molecules are moving down their concentration and/or electrical gradient, which is a passive process that does not require an energy expenditure.

Co-transporters move two substances simultaneously. One of the substances is moving down its electrochemical gradient and the other substance is moving up its electrochemical gradient. The energy needed to move one of the substances up its electrochemical gradient is provided by the energy released as the second substance moves down its electrochemical gradient rather than by ATP hydrolysis.
