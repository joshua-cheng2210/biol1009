The most closely related species are those which share the most recent common ancestor. In the tree of life, forks represent a shared common ancestor for all the lines of evolution that subsequently branch from that point. However, in this example the most recent fork that has a line of evolution to X has only one other line of evolution, that leads to an un-named species that is extinct (we can see that it is extinct because its line of evolution ends before the present time). If we go back to the next most recent fork to which X is connected, we see evolutionary lines from that fork leading to W and V. However, W is extinct. Therefore, the extant (non-extinct) species most closely related to X is V.

An individual organism cannot evolve through natural selection. Only populations can evolve through natural selection.
