Habitat fragmentation leads to smaller population sizes. In small populations, genetic drift tends to cause a loss of genetic diversity, making the populations less able to adapt to changing conditions and therefore more likely to go extinct. Genetic drift can also cause undesirable alleles to become fixed in the population (i.e. for all members of the population to become homozygous for the undesirable allele).
