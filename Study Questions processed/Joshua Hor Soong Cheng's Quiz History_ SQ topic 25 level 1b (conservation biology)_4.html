In the case of toxic substances that are retained in organisms, it is safest to eat organisms at the lowest possible trophic level. In this example, that would mean that it is safest to eat the plants. The small fish that eat the plants would have a higher concentration of the toxic substance, because each fish would need to eat about 10 pounds of plant material in order to put on one pound of weight. So, the toxins that were present in 10 pounds of plant material would end up concentrated in one pound of fish. It would be even worse to eat the large fish. To produce 1 pound of large fish takes 10 pounds of the small fish on which the large fish feed. So, 1 pound of large fish would contain the amount of toxin present in 10 pounds of small fish, or 100 pounds of plant material. Eating the very large fish would be the worst. One pound of very large fish would contain the amount of toxin present in 10 pounds of the large fish, 100 pounds of the small fish, or 1,000 pounds of the plants.

Two species with identical ecological niches cannot coexist in the same community, as one species will be at least a little more effective than the other in utilizing resources. Over time, the species that is best at utilizing the resources will reproduce more quickly than the other species, and the less successful species will become extinct in that community. So, introduction of a non-native species that occupies the same ecological niche will lead to competition for resources between the non-native and native species. If the non-native species is more competitive, the native species will go extinct in that community.

Normal, clean water is actually somewhat acidic, as it has a pH typically between 5.0 and 5.5, which is below the pH of pure water, which is 7.0. Acid rain is more acidic then normal, clean rain, and has a pH typically between 4.0 and 4.5, although readings as low as 3.0 have been found. Remember that pH is equal to the negative log of the hydrogen ion concentration. So, a lower pH value corresponds with a higher concentration of hydrogen ions.
