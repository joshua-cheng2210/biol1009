According to the biological species concept, a species is a group of populations whose members have the potential to interbreed in nature and produce viable, fertile offspring, but that do not produce viable, fertile offspring with members of other such groups. So, according to this concept, an organism of one species is not able to produce viable, fertile offspring with a member of a different species. Therefore, biological species are reproductively isolated. The biological species concept cannot be used to distinguish species among extinct forms of life, as we generally lack information about which of those forms of life were able to breed with other forms and produce viable, fertile offspring.
