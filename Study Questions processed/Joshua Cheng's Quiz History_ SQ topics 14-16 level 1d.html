Good job!

Early during embryo development in females, the XIST gene becomes active on one of the two X chromosomes. The X chromosome on which the XIST gene becomes active is random, and is different for different cells in the embryo. When the XIST gene becomes active, it is transcribed and its transcript (RNA) attaches to the X chromosome that made it. Interaction of the RNA from the XIST gene initiates inactivation of that X chromosome. The inactive X chromosome condenses, forming what is known as a Barr body.

Good work!

The dominant trait is W. Affected individuals are represented by dark squares or circles and unaffected individuals are represented by open squares or circles. An individual (I-2) who is Ww is shown as being affected. Thus, we know that the dominant allele, W, causes individuals to be affected. Because IV-4 is not affected, IV-4 must be ww. IV-3 is affected, which could be due to being WW or Ww. We can determine that IV-3 must be Ww, because IV-3 has a parent who is not affected. This parent must therefore be ww, meaning that IV-3 must have inherited a w allele from that parent. Therefore, the cross is: Ww X ww. This cross will result in an equal mixture of progeny that are Ww (and are affected) and ww (and are not affected). Therefore, 50% of the progeny of IV-3 and IV-4 will be affected.

Nice work!

IB represents the B blood type allele, IA
represents the A blood type allele and i represents the O blood type allele. The genotype of the man with type B blood could be IBIB
or IBi, so his gametes could be IB
or i. The genotype of the woman with type A blood could be IAIA
or IAi so her gametes could be IA or i.
 
If the man is IBIB
and the woman is IAIA, all their children will be IBIA and will have the AB blood type.
 
If the man is IBIB
and the woman is IAi, half of their children will be IBIA
and will have the AB blood type and the other half will be IBi
and will have B type blood.
 
If the man is IBi
and the woman is IAIA, half of their children will be IBIA and will have the AB blood type and the other half will be iIA and will have the A blood type.
 
If the man is IBi
and the woman is IAi, of their children will be IBIA and will have the AB blood type, will be IBi and will have type B blood, will be iIA and will have type A blood and will be ii and will have type O blood.
 
So, from the information given, they could have children with type A, B, AB or O blood.

Primase catalyzes the synthesis of short RNA primers.  DNA polymerase III than catalyzes the extension of those primers, adding deoxyribonucleotides to the 3 end of the RNA primer and forming DNA in the 5 to 3 direction.  In E. coli, DNA polymerase I removes the RNA primers and fills in the resulting gaps.  DNA ligase catalyzes the formation of covalent bonds between the ends of adjacent DNA fragments (i.e. Okazaki fragments), resulting in formation of a single, long DNA molecule.
