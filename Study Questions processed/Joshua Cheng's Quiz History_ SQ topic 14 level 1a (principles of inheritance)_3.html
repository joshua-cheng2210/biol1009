Plants that are true breeding are homozygous for the gene that controls the character being studied, meaning that they have two copies of the same allele of that gene. The fact that all the F1 plants have red, axial flowers means that the red allele of the flower-color gene is dominant and the axial allele of the flower-type gene is dominant. If we represent the red and white alleles of the flower color gene as R and r, respectively, and the axial and terminal alleles of the flower type gene as T
and t, respectively, the cross the original parent plants was: RRTT
x rrtt. Therefore, the all the F1 plants will be RrTt. A cross between two F1 plants (RrTt X RrTt) will yield an equal mix of F2 progeny with the following genotypes for the flower color gene: RR, Rr, rR and rr. The of the plants that are RR, Rr or rR will have red flowers. The cross between two F1 plants will yield an equal mix of F2 progeny with the following genotypes for the flower-type gene: TT, Tt, tT and tt. The of the plants that are tt will have terminal flowers. Therefore, X = 3/16 of the plants will have red, terminal flowers. 3/16 X 1,000 = 187.5, which is approximately 190.
