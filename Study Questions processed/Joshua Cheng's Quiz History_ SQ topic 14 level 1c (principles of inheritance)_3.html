The progeny of a cross may or may not be heterozygotes.

Remember that “F1” stands for “Filial generation 1”, which is the first generation produced by a cross between two individuals of the parental (typically homozygous for different alleles of the gene or genes being studies) generation. In other words, if you cross parents “X” and “Y”, their children would be the F1
generation. If you then cross two F1 individuals, the resulting progeny would be the F2 generation, and would be the “grandchildren” of X and Y.

Radishes that are heterozygous for this flower color gene display a phenotype that is intermediate to the phenotype of radishes that are homozygous for either allele. Such an intermediate phenotype is characteristic of alleles that exhibit incomplete dominance. In contrast, “codominance” is when the traits of both alleles are expressed. For example, cows that are heterozygous for red and white hair color alleles produce some hairs that are red and some that are white. So, they express both the red and white phenotypes. If the cow hair color alleles were instead incompletely dominant, cows that were heterozygous for the hair color gene would make all pink hairs.

If the tall allele is represented as T and the short allele is represented by t, the cross is: Tt X TT. Half of the progeny will be TT and half of the progeny will be Tt. Because all the progeny will have at least one T allele, and the T allele is dominant and causes the plants to be tall, all of the offspring will be tall. Therefore, there is 0 probability of this cross producing a short plant.

Before Mendel’s work, many people thought that the traits exhibited by progeny were a result of the “blending” of the traits of the parents. Mendel’s work showed that this is not true. Instead, we inherit genes from our parents that determine our traits. In many cases one allele of a particular gene is dominant to another allele, so that progeny only exhibit the trait of the dominant allele. For example, children who inherit one allele for blue eyes and one allele for brown eyes have brown eyes, not eyes that are halfway between blue and brown.
