The logistic model of population growth assumes there is a maximum population size that the environment can support and that population growth slows as the population approaches that carrying capacity. In this model, the change in population size (dN) over time (dt) is represented by rN x (K N)/ K. In this equation, r represents the rate of population growth if growth rates are unlimited by population density, and N represents the number of individuals in the population. So, if growth rates were unrestricted (i.e. if growth rates were exponential), dN/dt
would be equal to rN. However, in this example the population growth is not un-limited, it is increasingly restricted as the size of the population nears the carrying capacity (maximum number of individuals that can be supported) of the environment. In other words, the population experiences logistic growth. The carrying capacity of the environment is symbolized by K. The effects on growth rate of the actual size of the population in relation to the carrying capacity are calculated by (K N)/K. If the size of N is approximately the same as K (i.e. if the number of individuals already present is close to the carrying capacity), then (K N)/K will be approximately equal to zero, and their will be little to no population growth.
