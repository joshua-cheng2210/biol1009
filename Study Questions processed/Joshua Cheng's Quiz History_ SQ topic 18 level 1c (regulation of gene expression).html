Good work!

Post-transcriptional regulation refers to regulation that occurs after DNA has been transcribed to form a primary RNA.  DNA modifications affect chromatin packing and the ease with which particular genes can be transcribed, so they are NOT examples of post-transcriptional regulation.  Similarly, the folding of DNA to form heterochromatin affects transcription rates, and so is not a form of post-transcriptional regulation.  The binding of transcription factors to a promoter affects binding of RNA polymerase to the promoter and thus transcription of the gene and is thus not an example of post-transcriptional regulation.  The removal of introns and alternative splicing of exons ARE examples of post-transcriptional regulation, as these are processes that occur AFTER a transcription to form a primary transcript has occurred.

The genes in the tryptophan (trp) operon encode enzymes that are used to make tryptophan.  So, cells only want to express these genes when the cells are low in tryptophan.  This means that cells turn off expression of the genes of the trp operon whenever substantial amounts of tryptophan are present in the cell.  The mechanism by which this occurs is that tryptophan itself is a corepressor for the repressor protein for the trp operon.  When there are lots of tryptophan molecules in a cell, the tryptophan molecules will interact with the repressor protein for the trp operon, causing the repressor protein to be stabilized in its active form.  The active repressor/corepressor complex will then bind the operator nucleotide sequence for the trp

Epigenetic inheritance refers to modifications of the proteins that form part of chromatin (e.g. acetylation of histone proteins) or of the DNA itself (e.g. DNA methylation, which means adding methyl groups to the DNA) that do NOT include changes in the actual nucleotide sequence of the DNA.  In order to be considered examples of epigenetic inheritance, these modifications must be heritable (i.e. able to be passed on when chromosomes are replicated and cells divide).
