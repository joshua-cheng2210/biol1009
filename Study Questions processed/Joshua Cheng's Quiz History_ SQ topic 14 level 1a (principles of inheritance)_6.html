Good work!

Plants that are true breeding are homozygous for the gene that controls the character being studied, meaning that they have two copies of the same allele of that gene. The F2 plants represented by box 1 are DD, and so are homozygous for the D allele. The F2 plants represented by box 2 are Dd, and thus are heterozygous. The F2
plants represented by box 3 are dD, and thus are also heterozygous. The F2
plants represented by box 4 are dd, and thus are homozygous for the d
allele. So, the plants represented by boxes 1 and 4 are homozygous and so will be true breeding.

Nice work!

IB represents the B blood type allele, IA
represents the A blood type allele and i represents the O blood type allele. The genotype of the man with type B blood could be IBIB
or IBi, so his gametes could be IB
or i. The genotype of the woman with type A blood could be IAIA
or IAi so her gametes could be IA or i.
 
If the man is IBIB
and the woman is IAIA, all their children will be IBIA and will have the AB blood type.
 
If the man is IBIB
and the woman is IAi, half of their children will be IBIA
and will have the AB blood type and the other half will be IBi
and will have B type blood.
 
If the man is IBi
and the woman is IAIA, half of their children will be IBIA and will have the AB blood type and the other half will be iIA and will have the A blood type.
 
If the man is IBi
and the woman is IAi, of their children will be IBIA and will have the AB blood type, will be IBi and will have type B blood, will be iIA and will have type A blood and will be ii and will have type O blood.
 
So, from the information given, they could have children with type A, B, AB or O blood.

Nice job!

Mendel studied alleles where one allele was completely dominant to the other. For example, the allele of the flower color gene that causes a purple color is completely dominant to the allele that causes a white color. So, progeny that inherited two purple alleles or one purple allele and one white allele looked exactly like the parent that had two purple alleles or a purple allele and a white allele (i.e. they all had purple flowers). Progeny that inherited two white alleles had white flowers, just like the parent that had two white alleles.
