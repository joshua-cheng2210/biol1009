A producer is an organism that produces organic compounds from CO2 by harnessing light energy (photosynthesis) or by oxidizing inorganic chemicals (in chemosynthetic reactions carried out by some prokaryotes). A primary consumer is an herbivore; an organism that eats plants or other animals. A secondary consumer is a carnivore that eats herbivores. A tertiary consumer is a carnivore that eats other carnivores (i.e. secondary consumers). A food chain begins with a producer. Next comes a primary consumer (e.g. a grasshopper), then a secondary consumer (e.g. a lizard) and then a tertiary consumer (e.g. a hawk).

Remember that, although exergonic reactions are also known as spontaneous reactions, the fact that a reaction is spontaneous does not mean that it will occur quickly or even that it will occur at all. Exergonic, or spontaneous, reactions are defined as reactions where the free energies of the reactant molecules are higher than the free energies of the product molecules. So, if the reaction occurs, there will be a net decrease in free energy. HOWEVER, for a reaction to occur the chemical bonds in the reactant molecules must be stretched into higher energy forms, so that the reactant molecules reach what is known as a transition state. The free energies of the reactant molecules in their transition states are higher than the free energies of the reactant molecules in their non-transition states. So, the reactant molecules may not have sufficient energy to achieve their transition states. If that is the case, the reaction will not proceed even though the overall effect of the reaction would be a decrease in free energy. Note that adding energy to some of the reactant molecules, so that those molecules are able to undergo the reaction, will result in a release of energy that may result in more reactant molecules having sufficient energy to surmount the activation energy barrier and undergo the reaction, leading to a further release in energy that allows more reactant molecules to undergo the reaction, and so on. In other words, once a reaction gets going, it may keep going.

Natural selection is based on the following: species produce more offspring than the environment can support, only a fraction of an individual's offspring may survive, there is heritable variation among individuals, and individuals whose characteristics are best suited to the environment generally leave more offspring than those whose characteristics are less well suited. However, poorly adapted individuals may still produce offspring. The difference between poorly adapted individuals and well adapted individuals is that the poorly adapted individuals will produce fewer (but usually not zero) offspring, and/or will produce less successful offspring.

Replication of double-stranded DNA molecules occurs by what is known as the semi-conservative model.  The DNA strands in the original double-stranded DNA molecule are known as the parental DNA strands.  The DNA strands that are made by copying the parental DNA strands are known as the daughter DNA strands.  According to the semiconservative model of DNA replication, each parental DNA strand is copied.  

So, after one round of DNA replication occurs, each of the two resulting double-stranded DNA molecules will consist of one parental DNA strand and one daughter DNA strand.  In the example above, after one round of DNA replication, the nitrogen atoms in each of the parental DNA strands will be 15N, whereas the nitrogen atoms in each of the daughter strands will be 14N.  So, each of the two double-stranded DNA molecules present after the first round of DNA replication will have on DNA strand made using 15N and one DNA strand made using 14N. 

When the second round of DNA replication takes place, the DNA strands made using 15N (i.e. the DNA strands made before the experiment started) will be replicated using 14N, so that the resulting double-stranded DNA molecules will consist of a parental DNA strand made using 15N and a daughter strand made using 14N.  These double-stranded DNA molecules will have a weight that is half way in between the weight of DNA made entirely from 15N and the weight of DNA made entirely from 14N.

Also during the second round of DNA replication, the DNA strands made using 14N (i.e. the DNA strands synthesized in the first round of DNA replication after the experiment began) will be replicated using 14N, so that the resulting double-stranded DNA molecules will consist of two DNA strands, both made using 14N.  These double-stranded DNA molecules will have the weight of DNA made entirely from 14N.

So, half of the DNA molecules will have a weight that is half way between the weight of DNA made entirely from 15N and DNA made entirely from 14N.  The other half of the DNA molecules will have the weight of DNA molecules made entirely from 14N.

The annual per capita birth rate is the average number of births in year per individual in the population. So, an annual per capita birth rate of 0.06 works out to: 0.06 births per year per squirrel X 1,000 squirrels = 60 squirrels born in the population in one year. An annual per capita death rate of 0.02 works out to: 0.02 deaths per year per squirrel X 1,000 squirrels = 20 squirrels die in the population in one year. Therefore, on average 40 (60 births 20 deaths = 40) squirrels will be added to the population in one year.
