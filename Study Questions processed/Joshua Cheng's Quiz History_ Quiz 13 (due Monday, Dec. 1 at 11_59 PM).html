By convention, dominant alleles are represented by a capital letter (R in this example) and recessive alleles are represented by lower-case letters (r in this case). The frequency of the dominant allele is represented by p and the frequency of the recessive allele is represented by q. Because the recessive allele codes for white flowers, we know that all the white flowering plants must be homozygous for the recessive allele, or rr. The frequency with which plants of the rr genotype will occur is represented by q X q, or q2. If 64 out of a total of 100 plants are white flowering, that means white flowering plants are 64% of the population. In other words, white flowering plants occur with a frequency of 0.64. Therefore q2
= 0.64, and q = 0.8.
