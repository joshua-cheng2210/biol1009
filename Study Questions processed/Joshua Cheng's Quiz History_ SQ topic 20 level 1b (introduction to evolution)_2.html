Natural selection is based on the following: species produce more offspring than the environment can support, only a fraction of an individual's offspring may survive, there is heritable variation among individuals, and individuals whose characteristics are best suited to the environment generally leave more offspring than those whose characteristics are less well suited. However, poorly adapted individuals may still produce offspring. The difference between poorly adapted individuals and well adapted individuals is that the poorly adapted individuals will produce fewer (but usually not zero) offspring, and/or will produce less successful offspring.

Convergent evolution is the evolution of similar features in independent evolutionary lineages. In other words, convergent evolution is when two evolutionary lines evolve similar features (e.g. structures) independently. In this example, dolphins and sharks do not share a recent common ancestor (one is a mammal, one a fish). Therefore, the fact that they have a similar overall body shape can NOT be due to them both having evolved from a common ancestor with a similar body shape. Instead, a more likely explanation is that their similar body form is a result of adaptations to their common environment, which exerts similar pressures on both species.

Remember that similarity in characteristics resulting from a common ancestor is known as homology. Convergent evolution is the process whereby organisms that are not closely related to each other independently evolve similar traits as a result of having to adapt to similar environments. So, the fact that the two species share many proteins and that these shared proteins are encoded by genes with identical DNA sequences would provide strong evidence that the two species evolved from a recent common ancestor, and that the similar-looking structure is thus due to homology.
