The fact that a cross between radishes with red and white flowers yields all-purple offspring tells us that the red and white alleles of the flower color gene (C) must exhibit incomplete dominance, so that a red/white heterozygote has a phenotype intermediate to that of red/red or white/white homozygotes (i.e. Cc heterozygotes have purple flowers). In contrast, the long allele of the radish shape gene (S) is dominant over the oval allele. All the F1 progeny from a cross between a plant that is true breeding (homozygous) for the red allele of the flower color gene and the long allele of the radish shape gene and a plant that is homozygous for the white allele of the flower color gene and the oval allele of the radish shape gene will be heterozygous for both genes.
 
Therefore, the cross between two F1 plants can be represented as: CcSs X CcSs. This cross will result in an equal mix of progeny that are: CC
(will have red phenotype), Cc (will have purple phenotype), cC (will have purple phenotype) or cc (will have white phenotype) for the flower color gene. So, of the F2 plants will have red flowers, will have purple flowers and will have white flowers. For the radish shape gene, the F2 progeny will be an equal mix of the following genotypes: SS, Ss, sS and ss. The of the F2
progeny that are SS, Ss or sS will have long radishes. The of the F2 progeny that are ss will have oval radishes.
 
Therefore, the probabilities for each phenotypic combination of flower color and radish shape are as follows:
Red long: X = 3/16
Red oval: X = 1/16
Purple long: X = 3/8 = 6/16
Purple oval: X = 1/8 = 2/16
White long: X = 3/16
White oval: X = 1/16
 
In order of most to least common: 6/16, 3/16, 3/16, 2/16, 1/16, 1/16. Therefore, the phenotypic ratio can be written as: 6:3:3:2:1:1

Replication of double-stranded DNA molecules occurs by what is known as the semi-conservative model.  The DNA strands in the original double-stranded DNA molecule are known as the parental DNA strands.  The DNA strands that are made by copying the parental DNA strands are known as the daughter DNA strands.  According to the semiconservative model of DNA replication, each parental DNA strand is copied.  
So, after one round of DNA replication occurs, each of the two resulting double-stranded DNA molecules will consist of one parental DNA strand and one daughter DNA strand.

Remember that C and G base pair and that A and T base pair. So, if the DNA sample is 42% cytosine, it must also be 42% guanine, because base pairing of cytosine and guanine requires that they be present in equal concentration. That means that 16% (100% - 42% - 42%) of the DNA sample must consist of A and T. Because A and T base pair with each other, they need to be present in equal amounts. So, the DNA sample must have 8% adenine and 8% thymine.
