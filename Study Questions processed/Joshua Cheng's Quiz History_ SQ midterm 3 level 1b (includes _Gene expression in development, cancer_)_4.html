The gene is on the X chromosome and the allele that causes white eyes is recessive. The F1 flies were the result of a cross between a mother that was homozygous for the red allele and a father that had the white allele on his one X chromosome. Therefore, all the F1
females inherited one red allele (from their mother) and one white allele (from their father). All the F1 males inherited one red allele (from their mother) and inherited a Y chromosome (rather than a 2nd X chromosome) from their father. As the red allele is dominant, all the F1
flies had red eyes. In the F2 generation, all the female flies inherited the red allele from their father, and therefore had red eyes, regardless of which allele they inherited from their mother. In contrast, all the male F2 flies inherited a Y chromosome from their father and either a red or a white allele on the X chromosome from their mother. Therefore, 50% of the male F2 flies, but 0% of the female F2 flies, had white eyes.

Cytoplasmic determinants can be polypeptides, mRNAs or other molecules that are distributed unequally in the unfertilized egg. This unequal distribution is maintained after the egg is fertilized. When the fertilized egg cell divides, the two daughter cells receive unequal amounts of different cytoplasmic determinants. These different cytoplasmic determinants cause different sets of transcriptional activators to be expressed in the daughter cells. In addition, cells may produce chemical that control (turn on or off) the expression of transcriptional activators in nearby cells in a process known as induction.

The normal function of tumor-suppressor genes is to decrease the rates of cell division and/or growth, or to function in DNA repair or cell adhesion. The normal function of proto-oncogenes is to produce polypeptides that increase the rate of cell division and/or growth. When the ribosome reaches a stop codon on the mRNA, the stop codon will be aligned with the A (aminoacyl) site of the ribosome. Normally, a tRNA with an appropriate anticodon binds the mRNA codon that is aligned with the A site of the ribosome. When this happens, the polypeptide chain attached to the tRNA in the ribosome P site is cleaved from the tRNA and the translation assembly (e.g. the ribosome, tRNA and mRNA) comes apart.

A gene that is producing its final product (protein or non-coding RNA) in a functional form is being expressed. So, because the final product of a gene that encodes an mRNA is a protein, a gene that is producing mRNA but no protein is not considered to be expressed. In contrast, the final product of genes that encode tRNAs and rRNAs are the RNA molecules, because these RNAs are not translated to form proteins. So, a gene that is expressing an rRNA or a tRNA is considered to be expressed.
