Nice work!

A plant that is homozygous for the “red” allele is crossed with a plant that is homozygous for the “white” allele to produce an F1 plant that is pink, and has one copy each of the red and white alleles. If two of these pink flowered F1 plants are crossed to each other, they will produce equal numbers of progeny with the following allele combinations: red red (will have red flowers), red white (will have pink flowers), white red (will have pink flowers), white white (will have white flowers). Therefore, breeding the F1
hybrids of the snapdragon cross produces F2 offspring, with a phenotypic ratio of 1 red: 2 pink: 1 white.

Nice work!

Roans are Rr. A cross between two roans is thus: Rr X Rr. This cross will produce an equal mix offspring with the following genotypes: RR, Rr, rR and rr. The ¼ of the offspring that are RR will be red. The ½ of the offspring that are Rr or rR will be roan. The ¼ of the offspring that are rr will be white. Therefore, this cross will produce offspring in the ratio of 1 red:2 roan:1 white.

Nice job!

This question asks you to consider 5 genes, labeled A through E. The individual in question has the same allele for two of the genes. In other words, the individual has two copies of the C allele and two copies of the E allele. So, all the gametes produced by this individual will inherit a copy of the C allele of the C gene and the E allele of the E gene. In contrast, the individual has two different alleles of each of the other 3 genes. For each of those three genes, a gamete can inherit either the dominant allele (shown with an upper-case letter) or the recessive allele (shown with a lower-case letter). So, the possible combinations of the alleles for those three genes are: ABD, ABd, AbD, Abd, aBD, aBd, abD and abd. So, this individual can produce gametes with 8 different genotypes.
