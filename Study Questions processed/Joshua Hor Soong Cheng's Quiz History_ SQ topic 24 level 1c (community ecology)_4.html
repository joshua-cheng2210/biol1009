Trophic levels are feeding levels within a community. A “producer” is an organism that produces organic compounds from CO2 by harnessing light energy (photosynthesis) or by oxidizing inorganic chemicals (in chemosynthetic reactions carried out by some prokaryotes). A “ primary consumer” is an herbivore; an organism that eats plants or other animals. A “secondary consumer” is a carnivore that eats herbivores. A “tertiary consumer” is a carnivore that eats other carnivores (i.e. secondary consumers). A “trophic structure”, or “food chain”, begins with a producer. Next comes a primary consumer (e.g. a grasshopper), then a secondary consumer (e.g. a lizard) and then a tertiary consumer (e.g. a hawk).
