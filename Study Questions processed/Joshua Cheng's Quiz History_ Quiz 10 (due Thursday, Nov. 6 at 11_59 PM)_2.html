Replication of double-stranded DNA molecules occurs by what is known as the semi-conservative model.  The DNA strands in the original double-stranded DNA molecule are known as the parental DNA strands.  The DNA strands that are made by copying the parental DNA strands are known as the daughter DNA strands.  According to the semiconservative model of DNA replication, each parental DNA strand is copied.  
So, after one round of DNA replication occurs, each of the two resulting double-stranded DNA molecules will consist of one parental DNA strand and one daughter DNA strand.

Replication of double-stranded DNA molecules occurs by what is known as the semi-conservative model.  The DNA strands in the original double-stranded DNA molecule are known as the parental DNA strands.  The DNA strands that are made by copying the parental DNA strands are known as the daughter DNA strands.  According to the semiconservative model of DNA replication, each parental DNA strand is copied.  

So, after one round of DNA replication occurs, each of the two resulting double-stranded DNA molecules will consist of one parental DNA strand and one daughter DNA strand.  In the example above, after one round of DNA replication, the nitrogen atoms in each of the parental DNA strands will be 15N, whereas the nitrogen atoms in each of the daughter strands will be 14N.  So, each of the two double-stranded DNA molecules present after the first round of DNA replication will have on DNA strand made using 15N and one DNA strand made using 14N. 

When the second round of DNA replication takes place, the DNA strands made using 15N (i.e. the DNA strands made before the experiment started) will be replicated using 14N, so that the resulting double-stranded DNA molecules will consist of a parental DNA strand made using 15N and a daughter strand made using 14N.  These double-stranded DNA molecules will have a weight that is half way in between the weight of DNA made entirely from 15N and the weight of DNA made entirely from 14N.

Also during the second round of DNA replication, the DNA strands made using 14N (i.e. the DNA strands synthesized in the first round of DNA replication after the experiment began) will be replicated using 14N, so that the resulting double-stranded DNA molecules will consist of two DNA strands, both made using 14N.  These double-stranded DNA molecules will have the weight of DNA made entirely from 14N.

So, half of the DNA molecules will have a weight that is half way between the weight of DNA made entirely from 15N and DNA made entirely from 14N.  The other half of the DNA molecules will have the weight of DNA molecules made entirely from 14N.
