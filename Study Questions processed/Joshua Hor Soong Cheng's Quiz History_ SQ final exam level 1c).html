An individual organism cannot evolve through natural selection. Only populations can evolve through natural selection. This is because evolution can be defined as the change in the genetic composition of a population from generation to generation. An individual does not change its genetic composition via natural selection during its lifetime.

Good work!

The amino acid will act as a corepressor.  Remember that corepressors (and inducers) are NOT named because of how they affect the activity of the repressor protein.  Instead, they are named because of how they affect the rate of transcription of the genes in the operon(s) they help regulate. Corepressors stabilize the active form of the repressor proteins with which they interact.  So, when the correct corepressor is present in a cell, the repressor protein with which it interacts will bind to it and be stabilized in its active form.  The active repressor/corepressor complex will then bind the operator sequence, preventing transcription of the genes in the operon by RNA polymerase.  So, the name corepressor refers to the fact that corepressors help repress transcription of the genes in the operon.

Also, think about the fact that a cell only wants to make the enzymes that allow it to synthesize a particular amino acid when the cell has only low amounts of that amino acid.  So, cells want to be set up in such a way that the presence of significant amounts of that amino acid represses transcription of the genes that encode the enzymes for making more of that amino acid.  So, you want the amino acid to act as a corepressor.
