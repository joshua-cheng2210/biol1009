Good work!

The fact that a cross between radishes with red and white flowers yields all-purple offspring tells us that the red and white alleles of the flower color gene must exhibit incomplete dominance, so that a red/white heterozygote has a phenotype intermediate to that of red/red or white/white homozygotes. Therefore, all the F1 progeny from a cross between a plant that is true breeding (homozygous) for the red allele and a plant that is homozygous for the white allele will be heterozygous for the flower color gene, and therefore purple. In contrast, the long allele of the radish shape gene is dominant over the oval allele. Therefore, a cross between a plant that is homozygous for the long allele and a plant that is homozygous for the oval allele will result in all F1 progeny being heterozygous for the radish shape gene, and thus having long radishes. Therefore, all the F1
progeny will have long, purple radishes.

A monohybrid cross is a cross between two organisms that are both heterozygous for a particular genetic locus (e.g., gene). For example, Bb X Bb would be a monohybrid cross as both individuals are heterozygous for a single locus (the B locus) that is being studied. A dihybrid cross is a cross between two organisms that are both heterozygous as the same two loci (e.g., genes).
