In this case, the concentration of glucose is the same on both sides of the tube. So, the concentration of free water molecules is the same on both sides of the tube. Nice job!

All types of diffusion are passive processes where molecules move down their concentration and/or electrical gradients.  Transport by cotransporters is a form of active transport.  Cotransporters work by transporting two different molecules together.  One of the molecules being transported is moving down its concentration and/or electrical gradient.  The other molecule being transported is moving up its concentration and/or electrical gradient.  The energy required to move the molecule that’s moving up its concentration and/or electrical gradient is provided by the molecule moving down its concentration and/or electrical gradient.  Example: A cotransporter moving H+ down its electrochemical gradient while moving sucrose up its concentration gradient is using the potential energy that is stored in the form H+ moving down its electrochemical gradient to do the work of moving sucrose up its concentration gradient.
