Eukaryotic transcriptional activators get their name from the fact that they activate (increase) transcription of the genes they regulate. They do this by increasing the affinity of RNA polymerase for the promoter(s) of the gene(s) they regulate. In contrast, transcriptional repressors get their name from the fact that they repress (decrease) transcription of the genes they regulate. They do this by decreasing the affinity of RNA polymerase for the promoter(s) of the gene(s) they regulate. RNA polymerase, not ribosomes, bind promoters.
