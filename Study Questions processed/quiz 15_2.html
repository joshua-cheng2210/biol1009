Water molecules are actually quite polar because oxygen atoms are a lot more electronegative than hydrogen atoms. As a result, the electron pair that forms the covalent bond between a hydrogen atom and an oxygen atom spends a lot more time near the oxygen nucleus than near the hydrogen nucleus. So, the bond between these two atoms is a polar bond and the oxygen atom carries a partial negative charge and the hydrogen atoms carry partial positive charges, making water a polar molecule.

Lipid molecules are composed primarily of hydrocarbons. C-H bonds are nonpolar because carbon atoms are only a little more electronegative than hydrogen atoms.

Carrying capacity will change as environmental conditions change. For example, if a drought causes the death of most of the plants on which a particular animal species feeds, the carrying capacity of that environment for that animal species may be significantly decreased. The logistic growth model allows an estimate of growth rates as a function of how close the population is to its carrying capacity, but does not provide an estimate of carrying capacity. Carrying capacities can be somewhat exceeded on a temporary basis. For example, a wolf population may continue to grow and eat all the deer, until the wolf population has exceeded its carrying capacity. However, within a short period of time this situation would likely lead to the deaths of many of the wolves, as they starved to death.
